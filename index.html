My Data Science Projects

Explore my diverse range of captivating data science projects, covering a broad spectrum of concepts including data analysis, natural language processing (NLP), statistical analysis, and machine learning.

Unveiling Insights into Crime Dynamics in the UK

This project utilised publicly available time series data sourced online to investigate current and forecast future crime trends in the United Kingdom. Employing big data technologies and intuitive visualization methods, it aimed to offer comprehensive insights into the dynamics of crime patterns for enhanced understanding and prediction.

Languages & frameworks: Python, Azure, Spark, Pandas, Matplotlib.

Hybrid Recommendation Systems

This project utilises a large dataset of customer reviews to develop and compare different hybrid recommendation systems. The goal is to assist online shoppers in making informed choices by analysing ratings and sentiments from other customers. Furthermore, a specific aspect of the project focuses on extracting keywords that reflect customer satisfaction with a product.

Languages & frameworks: Python, Transformers, Scikit-learn, NLTK, Matplotlib.

Analyzing Traffic Congestion Along the M1 Corridor (Junctions J1 to J48)

By leveraging publicly accessible data collected online, this project focused on analysing traffic patterns along the M1 road. Through the application of statistical methods, it sought to identify the most favorable times for travel. The insights gained could offer valuable advantages to logistics firms aiming to optimise their transportation logistics.

Languages & frameworks: R programming, ggplot2, Dplyr.

Comparing Various Deep Learning Algorithms for Breast Cancer Image Classification

This project utilised publicly available image datasets to examine and compare the performance of multiple deep learning algorithms, including a pre-trained model. It focuses on accurately classifying cancer images into IDC+ and IDC- categories.

Languages & frameworks: Python, scikit-learn, keras, matplotlib.
